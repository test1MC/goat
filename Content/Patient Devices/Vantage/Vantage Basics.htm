Vantage Basics

Charging the InTouch Vantage

The InTouch Vantage should be plugged in and left powered on at all times. Plug InTouch Vantage into a grounded “Hospital Grade” electrical outlet:

  • 120 VAC, 60 Hz, 15 amp (North America), or
  • 230 VAC, 50 Hz, 10 amp (International), or
  • 230 VAC, 60 Hz, 10 amp (International).

The system will charge to:

  • 80% in four hours.
  • 100% in six hours.
WARNING: The InTouch Vantage contains sealed, rechargeable, lead-acid, gel-type batteries. The InTouch Vantage should always be plugged in to avoid deep discharge cycles that can shorten the battery’s useful life. Other than keeping the batteries charged by keeping the InTouch Vantage plugged in, no user maintenance of the batteries is required.

Main Power Switch Panel

The Main Power switch panel is located on the right side of the expansion bay near the Privacy Handset. The Main Power switch panel contains the Main Power button, the Low-Power Alarm Reset button, and the Battery Charge Indicator Lights.

The Main Power button turns on the power to the InTouch Vantage and the Auxiliary AC Power Receptacle. If the InTouch Vantage is not plugged in, the power is provided from the battery.

The Battery Charge Indicator Lights indicate the remaining percentage of battery life whenever the InTouch Vantage is plugged in or turned on.

  • A fully charged InTouch Vantage V1 battery will last about one hour unplugged, and a fully charged InTouch Vantage V2 battery will last about 5 hours unplugged.
  • If the battery charge drops below the alarm threshold, the low-power alarm will start beeping. Press the alarm reset button to turn off the alarm, and plug in the InTouch Vantage as soon as possible.

Warnings:

  • The InTouch Vantage contains sealed, rechargeable, lead-acid, gel-type batteries. The InTouch Vantage should always be plugged in to avoid deep discharge cycles that can shorten the battery’s useful life. Other than keeping the batteries charged by keeping the InTouch Vantage plugged in, no user maintenance of the batteries is required.
  • Immediately report any damage to the InTouch Vantage power plug to Technical Service. InTouch Vantage V1 can be operated using the onboard battery system for about one hour and InTouch Vantage V2 can be operated using the onboard battery system for about five hours.

Power Controls

The InTouch Vantage has two power buttons:

  • the Main power button on the Expansion Bay that controls power from either the wall plug or the batteries, and
  • the Button Panel power button which controls only the computer.
