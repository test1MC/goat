Installation

Unpacking

The should arrive in cardboard wrapped around a wooden packaging pallet. The will be strapped down and immobilized using banding equipment and plastic wrap.

Remove the cardboard packaging from the wooden pallet. The cardboard is secured by drywall screws and washers.

Using scissors, carefully loosen the banding straps and plastic wrap.

Carefully remove the from the pallet.

--

Move the by holding the handle with one hand and placing your other hand on the metal tube attaching the head to the cart. This will allow you to have the best control of the when moving it. Locks can be found on the front two wheels if the cart needs to remain stationary.

The is not MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging) safe and is not MRI compatible. The should only be used in locations where the presence of metal is not controlled.

Connect Uniterruptable Power Supply

Ensure the is unplugged and the is off.

Using a #2 Philips head screwdriver, unscrew the two M4x14 screws on the cover of the base and remove the cover.

Plug the large yellow battery connector into the Uniterruptable Power Supply (UPS) as shown in the photograph at the right.

Put the cover back on base of the and replace the screws to secure the cover.

Plug the into a standard grounded AC outlet and allow the system to charge for at least 6 hours to reach a full charge.

Power Controls

The has two power buttons:

the Main Power button on the Expansion Bay that controls power from either the wall plug or the batteries, and

the Button Panel power button which controls only the computer.

The should be plugged in and left powered on at all times.

The contains sealed, rechargeable, lead-acid, gel-type batteries. The should always be plugged in to avoid deep discharge cycles that can shorten the battery’s useful life. Other than keeping the batteries charged by keeping the plugged in, no user maintenance of the batteries is required.

Power On Sequence

Plug in the power cord:

Ensure that the power cord is plugged into an
approved wall socket.

Turn On the Main Power at the Expansion Bay:

Press and hold (3-5 sec.) the main power button.

Ensure that the power light is lit.

Turn On the Power at the Button Panel:

Press the power button once.

Check to ensure the Display powers up successfully.

 

 

To ensure system readiness, connectivity, and charged batteries, power on the at least two hours before its intended use. This will allow the to check for and install any available software updates.

Power Off Sequence

When powering down the for any reason, always ensure that the Button Panel Power button is powered off first and that the Display screen turns off (turns black) before turning off the Main power.

The should be plugged in and left powered on whenever possible.

Turn Off the power at the Button Panel:

Press the button panel power button once.

Check to ensure that the display screen turns off (turns black).

It will take approximately 30-45 seconds for the screen to turn off.

Turn Off the Main Power at the Expansion Bay:

Press and hold the main power button for 3-5 seconds.

Ensure that the power light turns off.

Accessing the Core

The Core is comprised of a microprocessor-based system that runs Windows XP Embedded on and Windows 7 Embedded on . This system can be accessed to configure network settings or install new software or device drivers. You will need a USB keyboard configure the network settings.

Connect the USB Keyboard to one of the two USB ports on the Expansion bay of the .

Allow the system time to recognize that a keyboard has been plugged in. This should take no more than a few seconds.

Press the Spacebar twice.

The screensaver should disappear and be replaced with a window containing a white background with text showing several pieces of information.

Close this window by clicking on the X in the upper right corner of the window. If you do not see this X, press the spacebar one more time until it appears. You will see the typical Windows option on the upper right to minimize, expand or close out of the window, but it is important that you do not minimize this window. Just click on the X to close it.

After closing the window, you should see the Windows Embedded desktop.

By default, the taskbar is kept “Auto-Hidden” and will only appear by placing your mouse towards the bottom of the desktop.

Wireless Network Configuration

Start the Intel PROSet Wireless software as follows:
Start Menu -> Programs -> Intel PROSet Wireless -> Wi-Fi Connection Utility

Towards the bottom right, click on Profiles.
There may be some profiles already listed.

Click on Add.

A Create Wi-Fi Profile window will appear.

Enter a Profile Name that is descriptive of the connection or location.

In the field Wi-Fi Network ID (SSID),enter the exact name of the Wireless SSID that the will be using. The SSID can be obtained by the facility's IT department.

The radio button, Operating Mode: should be set to Network (Infrastructure) by default. Do not change this selection.

Click Next.

The next screen will allow you to enter security information for encryption and authentication.

Most IT departments will employ a method of security. This information will need to be obtained by someone in the IT department or have them enter this information while on this screen.

After entering the security information, click OK.

A list of wireless profile should appear and you should see the profile you just created.

If the profile you just created is not the first one in this list, do the following:

Highlight the profile you created

To the right of the list, click on the arrow that is
pointing up in order to move your profile to the
top of the list.

Highlight this profile (if it is not already highlighted) and click on Connect.

You should now see that you are connected to the wireless network you created in your profile list. Close this window.

In the Intel PROSet Wireless Utility window, you should see information after the Network Name, Speed, Signal Quality and IP Address.

To test that you have an internet connection open up Internet Explorer as follows:
Start Menu -> Programs -> Internet Explorer

The InTouch Health website should appear by default. If not, please try entering another website and check that it loads in the browser window.

Close out of all the windows.

Start the robot application by double-clicking on the Robot.exe icon located on the desktop.

Additionally, you can find the Robot.exe application in the following folder:
C:\Program Files\InTouch Health\Companion\Robot.exe

After running the Robot.exe application, the screensaver should appear indicating System in Standby Mode.
