Touch-Based Driving (Windows 8 & 10)

Touch-based manual driving is accomplished with a vector key you can popup from the bottom of the Remote Camera view. When the driving mode is enabled the vector driving grid will appear showing an image of the footprint of the Patient Access Device and a speed/direction vector. The footprint will turn orange when in Nudge mode. Refer to “Collision Avoidance on page 1”.

The vector key has four buttons to control the motion of the Patient Access Device. When you stop driving, the buttons will minimize after about 10 seconds.

NOTE: The first time you press on one of the arrow buttons you will see a warning message that these buttons will move the Patient Access Device. Select Don’t show this again, to disable the warning in future sessions.

Slide Right

Press the right arrow button and drag your finger to any point inside the Remote Camera View window and the Patient Access Device will slide to the right.

Slide Left

Press the left arrow button and drag your finger to any point inside the Remote Camera View window and the Patient Access Device will slide to the left.

Both of the slide buttons will always slide to the left or right in relation to the front of the Patient Access Device, not to the direction that the Head is facing (the camera view). If the Head is facing backwards, the slide buttons will move the Patient Access Device in the opposite direction from what is intuitively seen on the camera view. The slide speed is always set to slow.

Driving Reverse

Press the back arrow button and drag your finger to any point inside the Remote Camera View window. The Patient Access Device will first rotate its head to face backwards, and then drive backward. The reverse speed is always set to slow.

Drive Forward/Turn

Press and drag on the forward arrow button, the Patient Access Device will first rotate its head to face forward, and then drive forward. The green vector indicates the direction and speed you are commanding.

 

NOTES:

  • Your finger must remain in contact with the screen within the Remote Camera View window to perform any driving operation on the Patient Access Device.
  • If the Patient Access Device is in contact with an obstacle, it must be moved directly away from the obstacle until its bumper is free. In some cases it may be necessary to override the collision avoidance sensors to move the Patient Access Device. See “Collision Avoidance on page 1”.
