Volume Control

It is important to note that local volume controls exist on both the Provider Access Software and TR-1 Stethoscope units. These volume controls affect ONLY the volume of the headset attached to them. Alteration of the volume control on the TR-1 Stethoscope unit will NOT change the volume on the your headset. This must be done at the Provider Access Software.

 

NOTES:

  • Audio routes through a completely separate channel from the Stethoscope sound, hence one can talk and hear normally through the Patient Access Device at the same time one is listening to the Stethoscope, if desired.
  • A common problem encountered when using the Stethoscope is that the Provider Access Software volume may be set too high. Due to the enhanced sensitivity of the TR-1 Stethoscope device, the volume control should only be turned about a quarter of the way up (to the 9 o’clock position). Refer to the TR-1 Stethoscope documentation for more information.
  • As with the volume control knob, separate Diaphragm & Bell controls exist on both the Provider Access Software and TR-1 Stethoscope units which affect ONLY what is heard through the headset attached to them. Refer to the TR-1 documentation for more information on the operation of the Stethoscope unit.
