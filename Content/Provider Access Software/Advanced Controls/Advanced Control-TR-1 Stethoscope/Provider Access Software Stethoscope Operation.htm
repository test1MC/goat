Provider Access Software Stethoscope Operation

When logging into an Patient Access Device (TR-1 Stethoscope installed), the Stethoscope tab shown will appear in the Advanced Controls area of the Provider Access Software Dashboard.

  1. Prior to launching the Provider Access Software, connect the stethoscope unit to your PC with the USB connector, plug in the power adapter, and plug the headphones into the Head Set jack.

CAUTION: Only use USB cables provided by InTouch Health.

  1. Log in to a Patient Access Device with a stethoscope installed, and turn on the headphones.
  2. Select the Stethoscope tab in the Advanced Controls area of the Provider Access Software Dashboard.
    • Ensure that the patient is in a quiet environment.
  3. To start the Stethoscope click on the Start button.
    • You should hear the sound from the Stethoscope in about one second.
    • Stethoscope volume and diaphragm/bell controls are located on the stethoscope unit.

Do not turn the volume up too high or too fast, the headphones will distort the sound if the volume is too high.

  1. When finished, press the Stop button.
  2. Turn off the headphones to conserve the battery.

NOTES:

  • When using the TR-1 Stethoscope, you can talk and hear normally through the Patient Access Device at the same time you are listening to the stethoscope.
  • The Patient Access Device software’s priority is to guarantee that sound taken in from the Stethoscope is reproduced with complete fidelity on the Provider Access Software side. To achieve this, a buffering scheme is used to guard against Internet delays. This means that the audio which is heard from the Stethoscope is accurate, but is not exactly real-time; it is lagging about one second behind.
