TR-1 Stethoscope Troubleshooting
Symptom Explanation Action
Provider Access Software does not display the Stethoscope Tab. Patient Access Device may not be configured with the TR-1 Stethoscope or Provider Access Software may not be current.

Check that:

  • Provider Access Software is connected to properly configured Patient Access Device with an attached TR-1 Stethoscope
  • Provider Access Software is version 20.31.xx or higher.
  • If problem persists, contact InTouch Health Technical Service.
Provider Access Software Stethoscope State shows “Timed Out.” Cables may be improperly connected or damaged.
  • Check Stethoscope power, headset and serial cable connections with no obvious cable damage.
  • Ask someone at Patient Access Device location to verify chest piece is connected to proper socket with no obvious cable damage.
  • Restart your computer.
  • If problem persists, contact InTouch Health Technical Service.
No sound from the headset but the Provider Access Software Stethoscope State shows “Playing”. Volume control button on the Stethoscope unit may be turned down too low or cables may be improperly connected or damaged. Headset power is turned off or headset battery charge is too low. When a replacement headset battery is needed, use an AAA alkaline battery (IEC LR03). An AAA NiMH rechargeable battery may be used.

Check that:

  • Volume control button on Stethoscope unit is set in the 9 o’clock position.
  • Headset is connected to proper socket on Stethoscope unit.
  • Stethoscope unit is powered via power adapter.
  • Stethoscope unit is connected to your computer.
  • Make sure the headset power is switched on and the power switch indicator is illuminated. If the power switch indicator starts flashing the battery is in a low- charge state. Replace the headset battery.
  • Ask someone at Patient Access Device location to verify chest piece is connected to chest piece socket on the Stethoscope unit.
  • If problem persists, contact InTouch Health Technical Service.
Sound from the headset appears “noisy” or unclear. Volume control button on the Stethoscope unit may be turned up too high and picking up ambient noise near the Patient Access Device. Check that volume control button on Stethoscope unit attached to your headset is set in the 9 o’clock position.
Sound from the headset is “clipped” or drops out intermittently. When the power switch indicator starts flashing the battery is in a low-charge state. From the moment the indicator starts flashing, the battery has about four hours of life left. This remaining battery life may vary when using rechargeable batteries.
