Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What roles are a part of workflow?

There are three primary roles supported by the :

  1. Receptionist who schedules patient appointments, sets a physician’s availability, checks in patients for appointments, reschedule appointments, and creates follow-up appointments.
  2. Medical Assistant who is responsible for assigning a patient to an exam room, and recording their vitals, and notifying the provider that the patient is ready.
  3. Providers who are notified when the patient is ready so they can connect to the patient site and examine the patient. The Provider can also set their availability and see his or her their patient's appointments.

How many different service lines or specialties can be managed in my network?

There is no technical limit to the number of specialties or service lines that can be scheduled for a given network. The allows the management of multispecialty service offering.

 

How many different locations can be supported in a given network?

There is no limit to the number of telemedicine locations that can be supported by the platform for one’s health network.

 

Providers in my network often care for patients in multiple distant locations. Does the platform support coordinating care for these providers?

Yes, the platform allows providers to connect with patients at locations where they are licensed and credentialed. A provider will be able to see all patients he is scheduled to see that day from all locations on one worklist.

 

What if my health network has locations and providers in different time zones?

The supports all time zones.

 

Will I be able to access other InTouch apps from the ?

Other InTouch Apps can be access via the or directly from a Browser.

 

Do I need to download software to launch the ?

Currently, in order for a provider to securely connect with a patient virtually, they must log into the through the InTouch . However, outside of performing consults with patients, the can be accessed from any device that supports a web browser.

 

Can providers connect with patients on the using iPhone and iPad?

Currently, the platform requires that providers be logged into Windows on a desktop in order to connect to patients.

 

How many people can be in a physician consult at one time through the ?

Currently, a provider can only connect with one patient at a time at a specific location.

 

How will patients be notified of appointments?

There will be a confirmation and reminder email/SMS that will be sent to patients once appointments are booked, 24 hours prior to an appointment and 2 hours prior.

 

Does the product support scheduling for Call Panels?

Currently, the product does not facilitate call panel scheduling.

 

When will call panel scheduling be available?

Scheduling for call panels is a feature that is currently being developed. Although there is no specific release date for this functionality at the moment, it is expected to be available in the near future.

 

Does the Coordinator integrate into third-party EHR systems?

Yes, the will be able to fully integrate with customers current EHR and Scheduling systems.

 

How long will it take to fully integrate with third-party systems?

The is ready to be used “out-of-the-box” to see patients as soon as it is implemented but the time required for full EHR integration will be a continuous process and will depend case-by-case.

 

How does the support billing and insurance?

Currently, the does not record billing and insurance codes for visits. The is optimized to service telehealth blocks in a provider’s schedule which can be billed according to the appropriate telehealth insurance code for that block of time. However, the does integrate directly into the app which does support billing functions.
