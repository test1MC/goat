iPad Dashboard

The iPad Dashboard has five different areas: the Remote Camera View (video from the Patient Access Device camera), the InTouch Provider Access Software Camera View (PIP) (video from the iPad front-facing camera), the Tool Bar, the Tab Bar, and the Tab Display.

When the Remote Presence session starts, the iPad Dashboard will show the remote video from the Patient Access Device in the upper half of the iPad screen.

The video taken by the iPad front-facing camera will be displayed in a Picture In Picture (PIP) box at the upper right corner of the screen. The PIP box shows you how you appear to those looking at the Patient Access Device display.

Position the iPad so that your face is centered in the PIP box making sure your face remains centered in the PIP box during the remote presence session.

By pressing and dragging on the PIP box you can move it to the top center of the remote video display, to any corner, or you can tap the PIP box to close it. You can turn this PIP box off from the Settings Menu. See “Picture in Picture on page 1”.

 

A separate PIP box is also shown on the Patient Access Device display to show those at the Patient Access Device how they appear on the InTouch Provider Access Software display. You can turn this PIP box off from the Remote Controls section of the Settings Menu. See “Remote Controls on page 1”.

Landscape View

The land scape view on the iPad Provider Access Software allows for greater viewing area while allwoing access to the same controls as in the portrait view.
